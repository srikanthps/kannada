# What Are Vyanjanagalu?

**Vyanjanagalu (ವ್ಯಂಜನಗಳು)** are the **consonants** of the Kannada language.

Unlike vowels, consonants are normally pronounced together with a vowel.

In Kannada, every consonant automatically includes the vowel **ಅ (a)** unless another vowel sign is added.

For example:

| Letter | Read As |
|---------|----------|
| ಕ | ka |
| ಗ | ga |
| ತ | ta |
| ಮ | ma |

Notice that these are **not** simply **k**, **g**, **t**, and **m**.

They already include the vowel **ಅ (a)**.

---

# The Kannada Consonants

Kannada consonants are arranged into groups called **Varga (ವರ್ಗ)**.

Each group contains five related sounds.

This traditional arrangement is based on how the sounds are produced in the mouth and has been used for centuries.

# ಟ ವರ್ಗ (Ṭa Varga)

| Kannada | Transliteration |

| ಢ | ḍha |

```text

```

# ಅಂತಸ್ಥ (Antastha)

These are often called **semi-vowels**.

| Kannada | Transliteration |
|---------|-----------------|
| ಯ | ya |
| ರ | ra |
| ಲ | la |
| ವ | va |

---

# ಊಷ್ಮ (Ushma)

These consonants are produced with a stronger flow of air.

| Kannada | Transliteration |
|---------|-----------------|
| ಶ | sha |
| ಷ | ṣha |
| ಸ | sa |
| ಹ | ha |

Although **ಶ**, **ಷ**, and **ಸ** may sound similar to beginners, they are different letters and should be learned separately.

# Tips for Learning

Instead of memorizing all the consonants at once:

- Learn one group each day.
- Read each group aloud.
- Write each consonant several times.
- Review the previous group before moving to the next.

You'll soon notice that the five-letter pattern repeats throughout the alphabet.

# Did You Know?

The Kannada consonants are arranged according to **how they are pronounced**, not alphabetically.

The five **Varga** groups move gradually from the back of the mouth toward the lips:

1. ಕ ವರ್ಗ
2. ಚ ವರ್ಗ
3. ಟ ವರ್ಗ
4. ತ ವರ್ಗ
5. ಪ ವರ್ಗ

This scientific organization is shared by many Indian writing systems.

---

# Teacher's Note

Many learners worry about letters such as **ಖ**, **ಘ**, **ಛ**, **ಠ**, **ಥ**, **ಧ**, **ಫ**, and **ಭ**.

These are **aspirated consonants**. They are pronounced with a stronger puff of air than their unaspirated counterparts.

Don't worry if they feel unfamiliar at first.

With regular reading and listening, you'll naturally begin to recognize them.
