# What is Varnamale?

**Varnamale (ವರ್ಣಮಾಲೆ)** literally means **"Garland of Letters."**

It is the traditional arrangement of the Kannada alphabet taught in schools and used in dictionaries.

The Kannada alphabet consists of:

- **Swaragalu (ಸ್ವರಗಳು)** – Vowels
- **Vyanjanagalu (ವ್ಯಂಜನಗಳು)** – Consonants

Learning this order will help you use dictionaries, textbooks, and other Kannada reference materials.

# How the Alphabet is Organized

The Kannada alphabet follows a logical structure.

## Vowels First

The independent vowels appear before the consonants.

## Consonants in Groups

The consonants are divided into groups called **Varga (ವರ್ಗ)**.

Each group contains five related sounds.

## Scientific Arrangement

The consonants are arranged according to how the sounds are produced in the mouth, beginning at the back of the mouth and moving gradually toward the lips.

This organization makes the Kannada alphabet easier to learn than it first appears.

---

# Memory Tips

Instead of memorizing the entire alphabet at once:

- Learn the vowels first.
- Learn one consonant group at a time.
- Read the complete alphabet once every day.
- Write a few letters from memory.

Consistent practice is more effective than memorizing everything in a single session.

# Did You Know?

The order of the Kannada alphabet is very similar to that of several other Indian scripts, including Devanagari, Telugu, and Malayalam.

Once you understand the logic behind the arrangement, you'll notice similar patterns in many Indian languages.
