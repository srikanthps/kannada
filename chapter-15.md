# Kannada Numerals

The Kannada numeral system uses ten basic digits.

| Kannada | Arabic | Kannada Word | Transliteration |
|----------|---------|--------------|-----------------|
| ೦ | 0 | ಸೊನ್ನೆ | sonne |
| ೧ | 1 | ಒಂದು | ondu |
| ೨ | 2 | ಎರಡು | eraḍu |
| ೩ | 3 | ಮೂರು | mūru |
| ೪ | 4 | ನಾಲ್ಕು | nālku |
| ೫ | 5 | ಐದು | aidu |
| ೬ | 6 | ಆರು | āru |
| ೭ | 7 | ಏಳು | ēḷu |
| ೮ | 8 | ಎಂಟು | eṇṭu |
| ೯ | 9 | ಒಂಬತ್ತು | ombattu |

Read the digits aloud several times until you can recognize them without referring to the Arabic numerals.

# Reading Dates

Dates may be written using Kannada numerals.

Examples:

| Kannada | Arabic |
|----------|---------|
| ೦೧-೦೧-೨೦೨೬ | 01-01-2026 |
| ೧೫-೦೮-೨೦೨೬ | 15-08-2026 |
| ೦೨-೧೦-೨೦೨೬ | 02-10-2026 |

Practice reading each digit from left to right.

# Reading Addresses

House numbers are sometimes written using Kannada numerals.

Examples:

```
ಮನೆ ಸಂಖ್ಯೆ ೧೨

ಮನೆ ಸಂಖ್ಯೆ ೪೫

ಮನೆ ಸಂಖ್ಯೆ ೧೦೮
```

# Common Mistakes

## Confusing Similar Digits

Some Kannada numerals may look similar when you first learn them.

Take time to practise recognizing each digit individually before reading larger numbers.

---

## Reading Too Quickly

Read each digit carefully.

Accuracy is more important than speed.

Fluency develops naturally with practice.

---

# Did You Know?

Although Arabic numerals (0–9) are now used almost everywhere in India, Kannada numerals still appear in traditional calendars, educational materials, temple inscriptions, decorative artwork, and some official publications.

Learning them helps you appreciate both modern and traditional Kannada.

---

# Teacher's Note

A simple daily exercise is to write today's date using Kannada numerals.

You can also practise by reading clocks, calendars, and house numbers whenever you encounter them.

Small, regular practice is the fastest way to remember the numerals.
