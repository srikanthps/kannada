# Chapter 7 – Kagunita Practice (ಕಾಗುಣಿತ ಅಭ್ಯಾಸ)

← [Back to Table of Contents](README.md)

---

> *"The best way to master Kagunita is by reading every consonant with every vowel. Once the pattern becomes familiar, reading Kannada becomes much easier."*

---

# What You Will Learn

In this chapter, you will:

- Practise Kagunita for every Kannada consonant.
- Become familiar with the complete vowel pattern.
- Improve reading speed and confidence.
- Prepare for reading complete Kannada words.
- Build a strong foundation for learning Ottakshara.

Unlike the previous chapter, which explained **how Kagunita works**, this chapter focuses entirely on **reading practice**.

Read each row aloud several times.

Do not worry about speed.

Your goal is to recognize the pattern naturally.

---

# How to Use This Chapter

For each consonant group:

1. Read every row aloud.
2. Read slowly the first time.
3. Read smoothly the second time.
4. Read once more without stopping.

Notice that **only the consonant changes**.

The vowel order remains the same throughout the chapter.

---

# Vowel Order

Every row follows this order.

| ಅ | ಆ | ಇ | ಈ | ಉ | ಊ | ಋ | ಎ | ಏ | ಐ | ಒ | ಓ | ಔ | ಅಂ | ಅಃ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Once you become comfortable with this sequence, every Kagunita row will feel familiar.

# Reading Tips

As you practise:

- Read every Akshara clearly.
- Do not skip unfamiliar combinations.
- If you make a mistake, simply read the row again.
- Focus on recognizing the vowel pattern rather than memorizing each row.

Remember that the same vowel signs repeat for every consonant.

# Reading Tips

As you move from one consonant group to another:

- Keep the vowel order constant in your mind.
- Let your eyes recognize the entire Akshara.
- Read naturally without rushing.
- Repeat difficult rows until they become comfortable.

If one consonant group feels more difficult than the others, spend a few extra minutes on it before continuing.

# Daily Practice

To improve your reading:

- Read one consonant group each day.
- Repeat each row three times.
- Revisit difficult groups regularly.
- Read Kannada words whenever possible and identify the Kagunita forms.

Five to ten minutes of daily practice is enough to build confidence.

---

# Teacher's Note

Don't try to memorize every Akshara individually.

Instead, remember that every consonant follows the **same vowel pattern**.

As you continue reading Kannada, you'll begin recognizing these patterns automatically without consciously thinking about them.
